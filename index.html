Variational Dropout Sparsifies Deep Neural Networks
Dmitry Molchanov*, Arsenii Ashukha*, Dmitry Vetrov
ICML, 2017
talk (15 mins) / arXiv / bibtex / code (Theano, TF by GoogleAI, Colab PyTorch) / retrospective


Retrospective: i) SparsesVD indeed works. I have about quite a few practical use cases. However, careful usage of pruning often can produce better results. ii) Training of deep models with noise is known to be hard and unstable. That is less the case with SparseVD. All variances, by a happy coincidence, have been initialized with small values and did not change much during training. Using small constant variances does not hurt the performance, so SparseVD might be considered as a fancy regulariser with no noise. iii) The sparse solution is just a local optimum, as better values of ELBO can be achieved with a less flexible variational posterior q(w_ij)=N(w_ij | 0, σ_ij).

Variational dropout secretly trains highly sparsified deep neural networks, while a pattern of sparsity is learned jointly with weights during training.
